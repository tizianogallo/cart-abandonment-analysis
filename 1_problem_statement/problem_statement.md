# 🛒 Problem Statement

**Project:** Reducing Cart Abandonment at an Undisclosed E-Commerce Platform  
**Author:** Tiziano Gallo  

---

## 1. 🏢 Company Background

The subject of this project is an undisclosed multi-category e-commerce platform operating across electronics, appliances, and lifestyle products. The business serves hundreds of thousands of customers monthly through its online store, competing in a fast-moving retail landscape where digital conversion is critical to revenue growth.

> ⚠️ *The company name has been withheld. All analysis is based on publicly available behavioural event data published via Kaggle by Open CDP.*

---

## 2. 📊 Current Situation

The platform attracts a high volume of online visitors each month. Customer behaviour data collected between October and November 2019 recorded over **4 million user events** across three key interaction types: product views, cart additions, and completed purchases.

While the platform successfully drives product discovery — with a large proportion of users browsing and viewing items — a significant drop-off occurs between the cart stage and final purchase. Many users who demonstrate clear purchase intent by adding items to their cart ultimately leave the platform without completing the transaction.

---

## 3. ❗ The Problem

**The platform is losing a substantial proportion of potential revenue at the checkout stage due to high cart abandonment.**

Analysis of the event data reveals a steep funnel drop-off between cart additions and completed purchases. This pattern is consistent across product categories and time periods, suggesting the issue is systemic rather than isolated to specific products or seasons.

Key symptoms include:
- 📉 A large gap between users who add to cart and users who purchase
- 📭 No automated mechanism to re-engage users who abandon their cart
- ⏰ Abandonment patterns that vary by product category and time of day, suggesting friction points that are not being addressed

---

## 4. 💸 Business Impact

Cart abandonment represents direct, measurable lost revenue. Every user who adds a product to their cart has expressed purchase intent — their failure to complete the transaction is a recoverable loss, not an inherent one.

Industry benchmarks place average e-commerce cart abandonment rates at approximately **70%**. Even a modest improvement in cart-to-purchase conversion — for example, recovering **5–10% of abandoned carts** — would translate into a material uplift in monthly revenue without any increase in traffic or marketing spend.

> 📌 *The full financial impact will be quantified in the data analysis phase of this project.*

---

## 5. 🎯 Project Objective

The objective of this project is to:

1. 🔢 **Quantify** the current cart abandonment rate and its estimated revenue impact using transactional event data
2. 🔍 **Identify** the root causes and patterns driving abandonment (by category, time, and user behaviour)
3. 🗺️ **Map** the current checkout process (AS-IS) to surface friction points
4. 💡 **Recommend** targeted process and product improvements (TO-BE) that reduce abandonment
5. 📋 **Document** business requirements and user stories to support implementation
6. 📊 **Build** a Power BI dashboard to monitor abandonment KPIs on an ongoing basis
7. 💼 **Present** a business case with projected ROI for each recommended initiative
